Scientific References

This calculator is based on the following well-established guidelines:

  • ACSM (2007): Fluid replacement should aim to prevent excessive dehydration (>2% body weight loss). Fluid intake of 400-800 ml/hour is recommended. ACSM Position Stand on Exercise and Fluid Replacement
  • IOC (2019): Individualized hydration plans should consider sweat rate, exercise duration, and environmental conditions. IOC Consensus Statement on Sports Nutrition
  • Jeukendrup (2014): Carbohydrate intake of 30–60g/hour is recommended for endurance exercise, with up to 90g/hour for ultra-endurance events. Caffeine intake of 3-6 mg/kg can improve performance. Jeukendrup et al. (2014) - Nutrition for Endurance Sports
  • Shaw et al. (2014): Sodium intake should match sweat sodium losses, typically 500–700 mg/hour. Heavy sweaters may need up to 1200 mg/hour. Shaw et al. (2014) - Fluid and Sodium Balance in Endurance Athletes
  • Thomas et al. (2016): Fluid intake should be individualized based on sweat rate and environmental conditions. Sodium needs may increase in hot/humid conditions. Thomas et al. (2016) - Position Statement on Hydration
  • Goldstein et al. (2010): Caffeine doses of 3-6 mg/kg can enhance endurance performance, with a maximum of 200-300 mg/hour to avoid side effects. Pre-exercise dosing (30-60 mins before) is most effective. Goldstein et al. (2010) - Caffeine and Exercise Performance
